--- layout: post title: wildcards in ls command date: '2012-06-06T11:22:00.000-04:00' author: Xianjun Dong tags: - linux - ls - wildcards modified_time: '2012-06-06T11:22:10.654-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-12445049.post-6282198536680475122 blogger_orig_url: http://onetipperday.blogspot.com/2012/06/wildcards-in-ls-command.html --- To find all figures files in a current folder:

ls *{png,jpeg,jpg,gif,bmp,tiff,tif}

To find all bigWig files for factor CTCF and P300 in current folder

ls *{CTCF, P300}*.bigWig


For full details about wildcards of ls, refer here:
http://how-to.linuxcareer.com/learning-linux-commands-ls#h3-5-ls-command-and-wildcards



Wildcards are powerful feature of Linux shell. For more information enter:
$ man 7 glob
